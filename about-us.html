Before planting a crop, it's important to prepare the soil by adding organic matter like compost or manure to improve fertility and structure.
Depending on soil tests, necessary nutrients such as nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium may be added through fertilizers. Adjusting the pH with lime or sulfur ensures the right environment for plant growth. Incorporating cover crops or green manures can further enrich the soil, while ensuring proper water management is crucial for crop development. These steps collectively create optimal conditions for planting.

The water used for crops typically comes from several sources, depending on the farm's location and infrastructure.
Common sources include rainwater, which is captured in reservoirs; groundwater from wells tapping into aquifers; and surface water from rivers, lakes, or reservoirs.
Some farms may also use water from municipal systems. The choice of water source is influenced by local climate conditions, water availability, and the type of farming practices employed, all of which are essential for sustainable water management.
